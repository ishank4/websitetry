CBP&W stands for Cool Books, Publications, and Websites! Here are some of my favorite works below:

  • Check out this blog on evolutionary strategies by OpenAI! It includes some really interesting benchmark testing with reinforcement learning.
  • Play a game of chess with me! Invite FurkanToprak to a game on lichess.org.
  • Read this 2002 paper on NEAT (NeuroEvolution of Augmenting Topologies). NEAT is a novel Genetic Algorithm that mutates Neural Networks by mimicking reproduction in living organisms, especially crossing over in asexual reproduction. This is my favorite paper ever!
  • Here's a publication (Anderson 1986) that has some of the basics to back-propogation, reinforcement learning, and some benchmark performance tests like the pole balancing problem (Brownlee 2005).
  • Here are great books on Machine Learning:
    1. Hands-On Machine Learning with Scikit-Learn & TensorFlow by Aurelien Geron
    2. Deep Learning by Goodfellow et al.
    3. Machine Learning Yearning by Andrew Ng
  • Here are some interesting (in no particular order) papers on computation, memory, and algorithmic efficiency:
    1. The effect of local sort on parallel sorting algorithms by Jimenez-Gonzalez et al.
    2. Applications Know Best: Performance-Driven Memory Overcommit with Ginkgo by Silva et al.
    3. On Computable Numbers, With an Application to the Entschiedungsproblem by Alan Turing (1936). This was a headache to read. I am still unsure if I have completely understood it, but it was fascintating to read.
